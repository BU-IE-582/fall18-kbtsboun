In this figure, all bookmakers are considered as a unique bookmaker. To construct the figure, final odds (over/under) of different bookmakers are transformed to probability by deviding each individual odd (over or under) to total probabilty. Then, obtained probabilities represent estimation of bookmakers. In order to examine success of bookmakers, the obtained probabilities are devided into bins. Finally, empirical probabilities which are obtained from real results are compared with estimated probability according to corresponding binds.

In the figure, size of the circles represents cumulation of data point in a specific bin, therefore bigger sizes contain more significant information. Additionally, x=y line is the line on which estimations and real results meet. Subsequently, a small deviation from x=y line around 0.5 probability is observed which can be interpreted as bookmakers are making slightly wrong estimation in the favor of winner, because more than estimated number of match had finished over around 0.5 probability.
